Section 3.5 Introduction to Linear Transformations

Exercises Exercises

1.

(Source: [3], Chapter 6, Exercise 6.1.1)

Which of the following vector functions are linear transformations? Explain.
\begin{equation*} T_1\left( \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix}\right) = \begin{bmatrix} 2x + y \\ x-2y \\ -x-y \end{bmatrix}, \ T_2 \left(\begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix} \right) = \begin{bmatrix} x + y^2 \\ (x+y)z \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}, \ T_3 \left(\begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix}\right) = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}

2.

(Source: [1], Chapter 5, Exercise 5.2.9)

Consider the following functions \(T: \mathbb{R}^3 \to \mathbb{R}^2. \) Explain why each of these functions \(T \) is not linear.
  1. \begin{equation*} T \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} x + 2y + 3z + 1 \\ 2y - 3x + z \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
  2. \begin{equation*} T \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} x + 2y^2 + 3z \\ 2y + 3x + z \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
  3. \begin{equation*} T \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \sin(x) + 2y + 3z \\ 2y - 3x + z \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
  4. \begin{equation*} T \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} x + 2y + 3z \\ 2y - 3x -\ln(z) \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}

3.

(Source: [3], Chapter 6, Exercise 6.2.5)

Consider the following linear transformations \(T: \mathbb{R}^3 \to \mathbb{R}^2. \) For each, determine the matrix \(A \) such that \(T(x) = Ax. \)
  1. \begin{equation*} T \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} x + 2y + 3z \\ 2y - 3x + z \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
  2. \begin{equation*} T \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 7x + 2y + z\\ 3x - 11y + 2z \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
  3. \begin{equation*} T \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3x + 2y + z \\ x + 2y + 6z \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
  4. \begin{equation*} T \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2y - 5x + z \\ x + y + z \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}

4.

(Source: [3], Chapter 7, Exercise 7.1.3)

In each case, assume that \(T \) is a linear transformation.
  1. If \(T : V \to \mathbb{R} \) and \(T(\mathbf{v_1}) =1, T(\mathbf{v_2}) = -1 \text{,}\) find \(T(3 \mathbf{v_1} - 5 \mathbf{v_2}).\)
  2. If \(T : V \to \mathbb{R} \) and \(T(\mathbf{v_1}) =2, T(\mathbf{v_2}) = -3 \text{,}\) find \(T(3 \mathbf{v_1} + 2 \mathbf{v_2}).\)
  3. If \(T : \mathbb{R}^2 \to \mathbb{R}^2 \) and \(T\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}, \ T\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}, \) find \(T\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}. \)
  4. If \(T : \mathbb{R}^2 \to \mathbb{R}^2 \) and \(T\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}, \ T\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}, \) find \(T\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -7 \end{bmatrix}. \)

5.

(Source: [3], Chapter 6, Exercise 6.3.1)

Find the matrix of the linear transformation that rotates every vector of \(\mathbb{R}^2 \) by an angle of \(\pi/3 \) (counterclockwise).

6.

(Source: [3], Chapter 6, Exercise 6.3.2)

Find the matrix of the linear transformation that reflects every vectors of \(\mathbb{R}^2 \) about the \(x-\)axis.

7.

(Source: [3], Chapter 6, Exercise 6.3.3)

Find the matrix of the linear transformation that reflects every vectors of \(\mathbb{R}^2 \) about the line \(y = -x\text{.}\)

8.

(Source: [3], Chapter 6, Exercise 6.3.4)

Find the matrix of the linear transformation that stretches \(\mathbb{R}^2 \) by a factor of 3 in the vertical direction.

9.

(Source: [1], Chapter 5, Exercise 5.2.11)

Find the matrix for \(T(\mathbf{w}) = proj_{\mathbf{v}}(\mathbf{w}) \) where \(v = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -2 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}. \)

10.

(Source: [1], Chapter 5, Exercise 5.3.2)

Let \(T \) be a linear transformation induced by the matrix \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \amp 1 \\ -1 \amp 2 \end{bmatrix} \) and \(S \) a linear transformation induced by \(B = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \amp -2 \\ 4 \amp 2 \end{bmatrix}. \) Find the matrix of \(S \circ T \) and find \((S \circ T )(\mathbf{x}) \) for \(\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}. \)

11.

(Source: [3], Chapter 6, Exercise 6.4.4)

Find the matrix of the linear transformation that reflects every vector in \(\mathbb{R}^2 \) about the \(x\)-axis and then rotates by an angle of \(\pi/4. \)

12.

(Source: [3], Chapter 6, Exercise 6.4.3)

Find the matrix of the linear transformation that rotates every vector in \(\mathbb{R}^2 \) by an angle of \(\pi/6 \) and then reflects about the \(x\)-axis followed by a reflection about the \(y\)-axis.

13.

(Source: [3], Chapter 6, Exercise 6.3.7)

Describe the linear transformation that is given by each of the following matrices. Draw a before-and-after picture for each.
  1. \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp -1 \\ 1 \amp 1 \end{bmatrix}.\)
  2. \(B = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \amp 2 \\ 1 \amp 0 \end{bmatrix}.\)
  3. \(C = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 0 \\ -1 \amp 1 \end{bmatrix}.\)
  4. \(D = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 0\\ 0 \amp 0 \end{bmatrix}.\)
