Item 2.
Suppose that \(\begin{bmatrix}x_1\\y_1\end{bmatrix}\) and \(\begin{bmatrix}x_2\\y_2\end{bmatrix}\) are in \(S\text{.}\) Then \(y_1 = 3x_1\) and \(y_2=3x_2\) (by definition of \(S\)), so \(y_1+y_2 = 3x_1+3x_2 = 3(x_1+x_2)\text{.}\) Therefore the vector \(\begin{bmatrix}x_1\\y_1\end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}x_2\\y_2\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}x_1+x_2\\y_1+y_2\end{bmatrix}\) is in \(S\text{.}\)
