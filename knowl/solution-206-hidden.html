We could use exactly the technique that we used in ExampleÂ 6.2.4, but now we have a more efficient way. Let \(A = \begin{bmatrix}1 \amp 2 \amp 1 \\ 2 \amp 0 \amp -3\end{bmatrix}\text{.}\) Then \(S = \row(A)\text{,}\) so \(S^\perp = \row(A)^\perp = \null(A)\text{.}\) We already know how to find a basis for \(\null(A)\) - just row reduce!

\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix}1 \amp 2 \amp 1 \\ 2 \amp 0 \amp -3\end{bmatrix} \to \begin{bmatrix}1 \amp 0 \amp -3/2 \\ 0 \amp 1 \amp 5/4\end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Therefore vectors in \(\null(A)\) have the form

\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix}x\\y\\z\end{bmatrix} = z\begin{bmatrix}3/2 \\ -5/4 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}\text{,} \end{equation*}

and so \(\left\{\begin{bmatrix}3/2\\-5/4\\1\end{bmatrix}\right\}\) is a basis for \(S^\perp\text{.}\) If you don't like fractions we could always re-scale our answer and obtain the basis \(\left\{\begin{bmatrix}6\\-5\\4\end{bmatrix}\right\}\) instead.
