Let \(\theta\) be the angle we're looking for. Then:

\begin{equation*} \cos\theta = \frac{\vec{v}\cdot\vec{w}}{\norm{\vec{v}}\norm{\vec{w}}} = \frac{-2}{\sqrt{6}\sqrt{2}} = -\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\text{.} \end{equation*}

The angle is therefore the angle with \(0 \leq \theta \leq \pi \) with \(\cos\theta = -\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\text{.}\) This angle is approximately \(\theta = 2.186\) (radians, as always).
