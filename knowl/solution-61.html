Solution 3.1.3.1.
By definition of \(2\vec{v}\) being in \(\span(\vec{w}, \vec{z})\text{,}\) there are scalars \(a, b\) such that
\begin{equation*} 2\vec{v} = a\vec{w} + b\vec{z}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Therefore
\begin{equation*} \vec{v} = \frac{a}{2}\vec{w} + \frac{b}{2}\vec{z}\text{,} \end{equation*}
so \(\vec{v}\) is in \(\span(\vec{w}, \vec{z})\text{.}\) Therefore \(\span(\vec{v}, \vec{w}, \vec{z}) = \span(\vec{w}, \vec{z})\) by TheoremÂ 3.1.7.
