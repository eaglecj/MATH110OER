Exercise 6.

(Source: [4], Chapter 3, Exercise 3.3.21)

Suppose \(\lambda \) is an eigenvalue of a square matrix \(A \) with eigenvector \(x \ne 0 \text{.}\)
  • Show that \(\lambda^2 \) is an eigenvalue of \(A^2 \) (with the same \(x \)).
  • Show that \(\lambda^3 -2\lambda+3 \) is an eigenvalue of \(A^3 -2A +3I \text{.}\)
  • Show that \(p(\lambda) \) is an eigenvalue of \(p(A) \) for any non-zero polynomial \(p(x) \text{.}\)
