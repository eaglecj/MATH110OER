Item 3.
Suppose that \(\vec{v}\) is in \(\span(\vec{v_1}, \ldots, \vec{v_k})\text{,}\) and \(c\) is a scalar. Find scalars \(a_1, \ldots, a_k\) such that \(\vec{v} = a_1\vec{v_1} + \cdots + a_k\vec{v_k}\text{.}\) Then
\begin{align*} c\vec{v} \amp = c(a_1\vec{v_1}+\cdots+a_k\vec{v_k})\\ \amp = (ca_1)\vec{v_1} + \cdots + (ca_k)\vec{v_k}\text{.} \end{align*}
This shows that \(c\vec{v}\) is in \(\span(\vec{v_1}, \ldots, \vec{v_k})\text{.}\)
