Solution 1.1.4.1.
In the following, let \(\mbf{r}\) be the vector of the point \(R\text{.}\) In other words,
\begin{equation*} \mbf{r} = \mbf{p}+\mbf{q} \end{equation*}
by the choice of \(R\text{.}\)
  1. By definition, the components of \(\overrightarrow{QP}\) are the difference of the respective components of the point \(P\) and of the point \(Q\text{,}\) or in other words:
    \begin{equation*} \overrightarrow{QP} = \mbf{p}-\mbf{q}. \end{equation*}

  2. Similarly, we have

    \begin{equation*} \overrightarrow{QR} = \mbf{r}-\mbf{q} = (\mbf{p}+\mbf{q}) -\mbf{q} = \mbf{p}. \end{equation*}

  3. Just as above, we have

    \begin{equation*} \overrightarrow{RP} = \mbf{p}-\mbf{r} = \mbf{p}-(\mbf{p}+\mbf{q}) = -\mbf{q}. \end{equation*}

  4. If we write \(\mbf{0}\) for the vector of the origin \(O\text{,}\) all of whose components are zero, then

    \begin{equation*} \overrightarrow{RO} = \mbf{0} - \mbf{r} = - (\mbf{p}+\mbf{q}) = -\mbf{p} - \mbf{q}. \end{equation*}
