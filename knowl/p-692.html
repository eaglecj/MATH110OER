One direction is easy: If \(\span(\vec{v_1}, \ldots, \vec{v_k}) = \span(\vec{v_1}, \ldots, \vec{v_{i-1}}, \vec{v_{i+1}}, \ldots, \vec{v_k})\) then, since \(\vec{v_i}\) is certainly in \(\span(\vec{v_1}, \ldots, \vec{v_k})\text{,}\) we also have that \(\vec{v_i}\) is in \(\span(\vec{v_1}, \ldots, \vec{v_{i-1}}, \vec{v_{i+1}}, \ldots, \vec{v_k})\text{.}\)
