Item 3.
Suppose that \(\begin{bmatrix}x\\y\end{bmatrix}\) is in \(S\) and \(c\) is a scalar. Then by definition of \(S\) we have \(y=3x\text{,}\) so \(cy=c(3x) = 3(cx)\text{.}\) Therefore the vector \(c\begin{bmatrix}x\\y\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}cx\\cy\end{bmatrix}\) is in \(S\text{.}\)
