Solution 1.2.6.1.

According to the Cosine FormulaÂ 1.2.5.1, we have to compute the norm of the vectors and their dot product:

\begin{align*} \norm{\mbf{u}} \amp = \sqrt{\mbf{u}\cdot\mbf{u}} = \sqrt{ 1^2 + (-2)^2 + 1^2} = \sqrt{6}\\ \norm{\mbf{v}} \amp = \sqrt{\mbf{v}\cdot\mbf{v}} = \sqrt{ 1^2 + 2^2 + (-7)^2} = \sqrt{54}\\ \mbf{u}\cdot\mbf{v} \amp = 1\cdot 1 + (-2)\cdot 2 + 1\cdot (-7) = -10 \end{align*}

Therefore,

\begin{equation*} \cos (\theta) = \frac{ \mbf{u}\cdot \mbf{v}}{ \norm{\mbf{u}}\cdot \norm{\mbf{v}}} = \frac{-10}{ \sqrt{6}\cdot \sqrt{54}} = \frac{-10}{18} = \frac{-5}{9} \end{equation*}

and

\begin{equation*} \theta =\mathrm{arcos} \left(\frac{-5}{9}\right) . \end{equation*}
