Unfortunately, there is no way to calculate \(\norm{\vec{v}+\vec{w}}\) just from \(\norm{v}\) and \(\norm{w}\text{.}\) For instance, if \(\vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix}1\\0\end{bmatrix}\text{,}\) \(\vec{w} = \begin{bmatrix}1\\0\end{bmatrix}\text{,}\) and \(\vec{z} = \begin{bmatrix}1\\1\end{bmatrix}\) then \(\norm{\vec{v}} = \norm{\vec{w}} = \norm{\vec{z}} = 1\text{,}\) but \(\norm{\vec{v}+\vec{w}} = 2\) while \(\norm{\vec{v}+\vec{z}} = \sqrt{5}\text{.}\)
