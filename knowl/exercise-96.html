In each case, assume that \(T \) is a linear transformation.
  1. If \(T : V \to \mathbb{R} \) and \(T(\mathbf{v_1}) =1, T(\mathbf{v_2}) = -1 \text{,}\) find \(T(3 \mathbf{v_1} - 5 \mathbf{v_2}).\) Solution Answer
  2. If \(T : V \to \mathbb{R} \) and \(T(\mathbf{v_1}) =2, T(\mathbf{v_2}) = -3 \text{,}\) find \(T(3 \mathbf{v_1} + 2 \mathbf{v_2}).\) Solution Answer
  3. If \(T : \mathbb{R}^2 \to \mathbb{R}^2 \) and \(T\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}, \ T\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix},\) find \(T\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}. \) Solution Answer
  4. If \(T : \mathbb{R}^2 \to \mathbb{R}^2 \) and \(T\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}, \ T\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}, \) find \(T\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -7 \end{bmatrix}. \) Solution Answer
